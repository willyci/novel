# Volume 2: The Truth - Beat Sheet

> Chapter range: Ch6 - Ch10 (5 chapters)
> Core conflict: Peter discovers the real plan; moral crisis
> Volume climax: Peter inserts the bug

## 1) Opening Promise
- This volume promises: Peter's journey from oblivious employee to the most important decision of his life; the reveal of the radical group's full plan; the moment where one man's code decides humanity's fate
- Primary payoff types: Information (the 67M-year star chart discovery), Emotion (Peter's moral crisis), Cognitive (the reader finally sees the full picture)

## 2) Catalyst
- Event: Peter arrives at the Kuiper Belt asteroid base and begins working on the satellite navigation code
- Irreversible change: Peter is now physically inside the group's operation, isolated in deep space with no easy escape
- Protagonist's immediate goal: Complete the navigation programming contract and prove his skills still matter

## 3) Escalating Crisis Chain (Fichtean, 3+ escalations)
| Node | Crisis / Conflict | Stakes / Risk Escalation | Result / Change |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Peter notices the project is oddly secretive — no mission briefings, no client meetings, compartmentalized information | Low: professional unease; Peter rationalizes it as corporate confidentiality | Peter suppresses his doubts; his passivity (core flaw) enables continued compliance |
| 2 | Peter discovers the stellar coordinates in his navigation data are 67 million years old — these aren't current star positions | Medium: the entire project is a lie; Peter realizes this isn't asteroid mining | Peter's worldview shatters — "this isn't a mining company" |
| 3 | Peter pieces together the full plan: the nanomachines, the accelerator, the energy source, the time travel — they intend to deflect the Chicxulub asteroid and erase humanity | Critical: Peter is complicit in a plan to commit human extinction; he's trapped on an asteroid with the people who would kill him if he resisted | Peter faces the moral crisis: act or stay silent |
| 4 | Peter must decide whether to sabotage the code — knowing he'll have only one chance, no allies, and no escape if caught | Maximum: Peter's life vs. humanity's existence | Peter chooses to act — inserts the conditional bug |

## 4) Midpoint Reversal
- False victory: Peter completes the navigation code and feels professional pride for the first time in months — his skills matter, the code is elegant, the trajectory calculations are perfect. He briefly considers that maybe the project is legitimate after all.
- New knowledge: Then he runs a routine stellar calibration check and discovers the star positions don't match any current epoch. They match the sky of 67 million years ago. Everything he's built is designed to operate in the deep past.

## 5) Volume-End Low Point (All Is Lost)
- Low point event: Peter fully understands the plan and realizes he is the only person who can stop it — but he is alone, trapped on an asteroid in the outer solar system, surrounded by people who would kill him without hesitation
- Cost: If he acts, he risks his life with no guarantee of success; if he doesn't act, humanity ceases to exist
- Protagonist's choice: Peter overcomes his passivity for the first and most important time — he inserts the conditional bug that reverses +0.01 to -0.01 when the timestamp is in the past

## 6) Volume-End Payoff + Next Hook
- Payoffs:
  - The reader finally sees the full plan — all the pieces from Volume 1 assembled into a single terrifying purpose
  - Peter transforms from passive observer to active agent — the moment he writes the bug is the climax of his character arc
  - The "code as weapon" anti-trope is delivered — no fights, no superpowers, just one conditional statement
- Next hook: The nanomachine is about to be sent through the micro black hole — will Peter's bug work? What happens when it arrives 67 million years in the past?
- Final chapter unresolved question: Peter has inserted the bug, but the mission is launching — he must watch and pretend everything is normal while knowing he just altered the course of history
