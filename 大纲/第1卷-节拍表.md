# Volume 1: The Pieces - Beat Sheet

> Chapter range: Prologue - Ch5 (6 chapters)
> Core conflict: The heist — assembling stolen technology piece by piece
> Volume climax: Peter is hired; all pieces are in place for the time travel plan

## 1) Opening Promise
- This volume promises: a mounting sense of dread as disparate heist threads converge; a rich future world with moral complexity; the question "who is behind all this?"
- Primary payoff types: Information reveals, suspense escalation, world-building immersion

## 2) Catalyst
- Event: A broken glass tube in a Mars warehouse leaks self-replicating nanomachines — the first stolen piece
- Irreversible change: The nanomachines enter the black market pipeline; the heist has begun
- Protagonist's immediate goal: N/A (Peter hasn't appeared yet; the catalyst is the heist itself starting)

## 3) Escalating Crisis Chain (Fichtean, 3+ escalations)
| Node | Crisis / Conflict | Stakes / Risk Escalation | Result / Change |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Nanomachines stolen via Steve/Joe — a minor warehouse crime | Low: a broken tube, petty bribery (a hamster) | Stolen tech enters the black market; the mysterious man has nanomachines |
| 2 | Particle accelerator acquired under false identity (Dr. Freeman) | Medium: government-tracked equipment, identity fraud | The group now has the means to create micro black holes |
| 3 | "Sun 2.0" stolen from National Energy Lab — explosion kills a security guard (Mike) | High: a man is dead, government investigation launched | The group has the energy source; stakes shift from theft to murder |
| 4 | Peter, an unemployed programmer, is recruited under false pretenses | Critical: the final piece — a human expert who can be manipulated | All components assembled; the plan can now proceed |

## 4) Midpoint Reversal
- False victory: The news broadcasts in Ch5 make everything seem normal — SpaceNano thriving, the lab explosion "under investigation." The heist is invisible to the public. The reader realizes the radical group has succeeded in plain sight.
- New knowledge: Peter accepts the job believing it's legitimate asteroid mining work — the reader sees the trap closing around him.

## 5) Volume-End Low Point (All Is Lost)
- Low point event: Peter, desperate and broke, accepts a too-good-to-be-true job offer without questioning it
- Cost: Peter unknowingly becomes complicit in a plan to erase humanity
- Protagonist's choice: He chooses financial survival over suspicion — his passivity (his core flaw) makes him vulnerable

## 6) Volume-End Payoff + Next Hook
- Payoffs (matching opening promise):
  - The reader now understands the full scope of the heist — nanomachines, accelerator, energy source, programmer
  - The world is established: Mars society, AI displacement, black markets, eco-extremism
  - The mysterious man's identity remains unknown, but his network is revealed
- Next hook: Peter begins working on the satellite navigation code — when will he discover the star positions are 67 million years old?
- Final chapter unresolved question: Peter is now inside the group's operation — what will he find?
