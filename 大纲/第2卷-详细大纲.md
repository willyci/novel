# Volume 2: The Truth

> Chapter range: Ch6 - Ch10
> Core conflict: Peter is seduced by a brilliant cover story and the first real belonging he's felt in months — then his own expertise destroys the illusion
> Volume climax: Peter discovers the star positions are 67 million years old; the cover story crumbles; he realizes the group plans to erase humanity

## Volume Summary

Peter arrives at the radical group's secret base — an asteroid hollowed out in the Kuiper Belt, disguised as a deep-space mining R&D facility. He meets the crew: the Handler (his supervisor), the Professor (a charismatic ex-academic who runs the operation), Dr. Vasquez (accelerator physicist), a nanomachine programmer, a supply pilot, two student lab assistants, and a machine tech. Peter begins programming satellite navigation code. When the bizarre requirements — stellar drift correction over millions of years — raise his suspicions, the Professor delivers a brilliant cover story: they've invented time-traveling nanomachine mining. Send machines back millions of years to mine asteroids before competitors exist; the nanomachines spend eons mining and refining, then launch ingots on million-year trajectories toward Earth; orbital interceptors in the present catch the ancient shipments. Zero wait time. A trillion-credit monopoly.

Peter buys it. The cover story is technically sound, explains every component he sees (nanomachines, accelerator, energy source, his own nav code), and gives him purpose. He belongs. For the first time since Helix fired him, Peter feels like his skills matter. He works productively, bonds with the crew, and starts to believe.

Then his own expertise betrays the lie. Running stellar position checks on his trajectory calculations, Peter notices the star chart data is too specific — not "millions of years ago" but a very particular epoch. He runs the math. The positions correspond to 67 million years ago. The Cretaceous Period. And the trajectory target doesn't match any interceptor orbit — it aligns with the approach path of the Chicxulub asteroid. They're not mining. They're redirecting the asteroid that killed the dinosaurs. If the nanomachine deflects it by 0.01 degrees, it misses Earth. Dinosaurs survive. Mammals don't rise. Humanity never evolves.

The volume ends with Peter trapped on the base, knowing the truth, unable to tell anyone, unable to leave, unable to decide what to do. His belonging is shattered. His purpose is inverted. The work he was proud of is coding the extinction of the human race. He is paralyzed — and the decision of whether to act belongs to Volume 3.

## Constraint Trigger Plan
