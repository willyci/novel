# Volume 2: The Truth

> Chapter range: Ch6 - Ch10
> Core conflict: Peter discovers the real plan; moral crisis
> Volume climax: Peter inserts the bug

## Volume Summary

Peter arrives at the radical group's secret base — an asteroid in the Kuiper Belt, disguised as a mining outpost. He begins programming the satellite navigation system, believing he's working on an asteroid mining project. Over weeks of isolated work, he grows increasingly suspicious of the operation's secrecy, its over-resourced infrastructure, and the people around him. The midpoint revelation comes when Peter runs a routine stellar calibration and discovers the star positions in his navigation data are 67 million years old. He realizes the satellite isn't being sent to an asteroid in the present — it's being sent through time. He pieces together the full plan: deflect the Chicxulub asteroid by 0.01 degrees, prevent the dinosaur extinction, and erase human evolution. Trapped and alone, Peter must decide whether to act. He overcomes his defining flaw — passivity — and inserts a conditional bug that reverses the trajectory. The volume ends with Peter delivering the code, his sabotage hidden inside, as the group prepares to launch.

- Cost: Peter's last connection to normal life severed — no external communications allowed
- Time anchor: 2543, Sol 30, morning (departure) → Sol 32 (arrival)
- In-chapter span: ~3 days
- Gap from previous: 8 days after Ch5 (Sol 22)
- Countdown: Code deadline D-40
- Satisfaction: Cognitive - the base is far larger and more resourced than any mining outpost should be (intensity 3); Emotional - Peter feels professional purpose for the first time in months (intensity 3)
- Strand: Quest + Constellation
- Antagonist tier: Medium (the Handler greets Peter; controls his access and information)
- POV: Peter
- Key entities: Kuiper Belt asteroid base (new location — hollowed asteroid, disguised as mining outpost), the Handler (the mysterious man from Vol 1, now Peter's supervisor — real name revealed or alias given), shuttle pilot, base personnel
- Chapter change: Peter is physically inside the conspiracy with no way out; he sees the three stolen technologies (nanomachines, accelerator, Sun 2.0) without knowing their true purpose

- Hook: Suspense - Peter is shown his workstation and given the project specs — the navigation parameters are oddly precise for simple asteroid mining

### Chapter 8: Ancient Stars
- Goal: Peter discovers the star chart dates to 67 million years ago — THE central revelation
- Obstacle: The discovery is so enormous that Peter's mind resists accepting it
- Cost: Everything Peter believed about this job — his purpose, his value, his safety — collapses
- Time anchor: 2543, Sol 50, evening
- In-chapter span: ~4 hours
- Gap from previous: ~7 days (routine work continued)
- Countdown: Code deadline D-20
- Satisfaction: Cognitive (MAJOR, intensity 7) - "These aren't current star positions. These are... these are sixty-seven million years old." Peter's expertise — the thing that made him unemployable in an AI world — is the exact thing that lets him see what no one expected him to see
- Strand: Constellation
- Antagonist tier: None (Peter is alone with the data; the danger is what he's discovered, not who threatens him)
- POV: Peter
- Key entities: Stellar calibration data (the 67M-year star positions), Peter's navigation terminal, the star chart comparison tool
- Chapter change: Peter knows the truth — this project sends something back in time. His worldview is destroyed. He cannot un-know this.
- Unresolved question: What exactly are they sending back, and why? Peter has the "when" but not yet the "what" or "why"
- Hook: Crisis - Peter realizes he's trapped on an asteroid with people who are building a time machine — and they don't know he knows

- Obstacle: The plan is philosophically compelling — the Leader's arguments are not easy to dismiss; Peter must find his own reason to resist

- Time anchor: 2543, Sol 52, night
- In-chapter span: ~6 hours
- Gap from previous: 2 days (Peter has been processing, pretending normalcy)
- Countdown: Code deadline D-18
- Satisfaction: Emotional - Peter's moral crisis is genuinely agonizing; the Leader's vision of a world without human destruction is seductive (intensity 5); Cognitive - the Leader's philosophy mirrors Peter's own despair about humanity's self-destruction (intensity 5)
- Strand: Fire + Constellation
- Antagonist tier: Large (the Leader appears — charismatic, articulate, terrifying in their conviction)
- POV: Peter
- Key entities: The Leader (group's visionary — eco-extremist who sees the plan as salvation, not destruction; addresses the base in a speech or private encounter), the full plan (deflect the Chicxulub asteroid by 0.01 degrees, prevent dinosaur extinction, prevent mammalian rise, prevent human evolution)
- Chapter change: Peter now understands EVERYTHING — the what, why, and how. He faces the decision: act or comply. His passivity is no longer neutral — it's complicity.
- Unresolved question: Will Peter find the courage to act? Can he sabotage the code without being caught?
- Hook: Choice - Peter stares at his terminal screen, the cursor blinking on the navigation function. He knows what he has to do. He doesn't know if he can.

### Chapter 10: Minus 0.01
- Goal: Peter inserts the bug and delivers the code
- Obstacle: He must write the sabotage without being detected, deliver the code as if nothing has changed, and maintain his composure while the group prepares for launch
- Cost: If discovered, Peter dies alone on an asteroid in the outer solar system; if he succeeds, he carries the secret forever
- Time anchor: 2543, Sol 65, morning → evening
- In-chapter span: ~8 hours
- Gap from previous: 13 days (Peter has been planning, working, waiting for the right moment)
- Countdown: Code deadline D-5
- Satisfaction: Cognitive (MAJOR, intensity 7) - Peter writes the conditional: `if (epoch < PRESENT_EPOCH) { trajectory_adjustment *= -1; }` — one line that reverses +0.01 to -0.01; Emotional - Peter's transformation from passive to decisive — the most important choice of his life, made in silence (intensity 6)
- Strand: Quest
- Antagonist tier: Large (the Leader and Handler are both present; the base is preparing for launch; tension is maximum)
- POV: Peter
- Key entities: The conditional bug (the story's central device), the completed navigation code, the mission launch preparations, the assembled technologies (nanomachines loaded, accelerator powered, Sun 2.0 primed)
- Chapter change: The code is delivered with the bug hidden inside. Peter has done the only thing he can do. The mission will proceed — and the group will not know until it's too late.
- Unresolved question: Will the bug work? What happens when the nanomachine arrives 67 million years in the past?
- Hook: Suspense - The Leader announces the launch window is in 48 hours. Peter watches the nanomachine being loaded into the accelerator chamber. He is the only person in the room who knows what's really about to happen.
