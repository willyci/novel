# 总纲

## 故事一句话
A radical group steals future tech to send a nanomachine 67 million years back in time to save the dinosaurs by deflecting the extinction asteroid — but a saboteur programmer reverses their 0.01-degree trajectory change, ensuring the asteroid still hits Earth and humanity survives.

## 创意约束
- 反套路规则：The hero wins not through combat or superpowers, but by writing a single conditional bug in navigation code
- 硬约束（世界/能力/行为）：
  - Time travel is one-way — no communication with the past
  - The nanomachine must operate autonomously for ~1 million years
  - 0.01 degree is the precision limit
  - Peter can only act through code, not physical confrontation
- 主角缺陷：Peter is passive, unemployed, demoralized — an unlikely hero
- 反派镜像：Both sides want to "fix" a broken world; they differ on whether humanity deserves to exist

## 核心主线
- 主线目标：The radical group assembles stolen tech to execute their time travel plan; Peter discovers the truth and sabotages it
- 主要阻力：Peter is isolated inside the group with no allies; the group controls all information

## 核心暗线
- 暗线主题：What makes humanity worth saving? (environmental destruction vs. human potential)
- 回收节点：Sarah's museum scene (dinosaurs as lost majesty) mirrors the final revelation (dinosaurs must die for humans to exist)

## 反派分层（概要）
- 小反派（前期）：Joe (middleman), Steve (unwitting accomplice), "Dr. Freeman" (false identity buyer)
- 中反派（中期）：The mysterious man in shadows (orchestrator, Peter's handler)
- 大反派（后期）：Professor Arthur Kessler — former evolutionary biologist, radicalized eco-extremist who conceived and leads the time travel plan from the Kuiper Belt base

## 世界观/力量体系简介
- 世界观要点：2543, Mars-colonized humanity, Earth in decline, Kuiper Belt mining frontier, AI displacing workers
- 力量体系要点：Technology tiers — nanomachines, "Sun 2.0" energy, particle accelerators, micro black holes; knowledge is the only "power"

## 复合题材融合（可选）
- 主题材 / 副题材：Hard Sci-Fi / Moral Thriller
- 融合机制（一句话）：Real physics provides the "how"; moral philosophy provides the "why"

- 关键冲突与爽点：The 0.01 → -0.01 reversal; the moment Peter discovers the 67-million-year star chart

## The Cover Story (First-Level Lie)
The radical group tells Peter they have invented a way to send self-replicating nanomachines back in time to mine asteroids millions of years before any competitors exist. According to their cover story:
- The nanomachines will spend eons mining and refining materials on ancient asteroids
- They will carefully launch the processed ingots back toward Earth on a million-year trajectory
- In the present day, the company has orbital interceptors ready to catch these ancient shipments
- This establishes an asteroid mining supply chain with zero wait time — a trillion-credit monopoly

Peter's navigation work under the cover story involves multiple scenarios:
- Programming ingot launch trajectories toward Earth from ancient asteroid positions
- Calculating asteroid approach vectors for the nanomachine's initial landing
- Adjusting for stellar drift over millions of years (which requires stellar cartography — his exact skill)

The cover story is brilliant because it uses all the same components (nanomachines + accelerator + Sun 2.0 + navigation code) for a plausible business purpose. Peter buys it — until he notices the star positions are 67 million years old, not "millions," and the trajectory target isn't an interceptor orbit but the Chicxulub asteroid's path.

## 主角成长线
- 起点状态：Unemployed programmer, passive, messy apartment, replaced by AI
- 关键跃迁节点：(1) Gets hired — has purpose again (Vol 1 end); (2) Accepts the cover story — belongs to something (Vol 2 mid); (3) Discovers the 67M-year star chart — realizes the truth (Vol 2 end); (4) Inserts the bug — chooses to act (Vol 3 climax)
- 终局定位：Silent hero — the man whose one line of code preserved human history

- The 0.01 → -0.01 reversal reveal
