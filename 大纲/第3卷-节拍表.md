# 第 3 卷：The Mission - 节拍表

> 章节范围: 第 11 - 15 章
> 核心冲突: Peter must overcome his paralysis, sabotage the code, and survive the aftermath — all while trapped on a base with the people he's betraying
> 卷末高潮: The mission launches, the nanomachine is sent 67 million years back, and reality refuses to change — Peter's bug worked, but now he's the only suspect

## 1) 开卷承诺（Promise）
- 本卷读者承诺：
  1. Peter finally acts — the passive man writes the most important line of code in human history (character climax)
  2. The launch sequence — all Vol 1's stolen technologies activate together (convergence payoff)
  3. The 0.01 → -0.01 reversal — the story's signature twist, delivered in full (cognitive payoff, intensity 8)
  4. "Nothing changed" — the group watches monitors and reality doesn't shift (dramatic payoff)
- 主要兑现类型：认知（the bug mechanism revealed）、情绪（Peter's transformation from passive to decisive）、结构（every foreshadow from Vol 1-2 pays off）

## 2) 催化事件（Catalyst）
- 事件：The launch countdown begins — Kessler announces the mission window opens in 10 days. The code must be finalized and locked. Peter's window for sabotage is closing.
- 不可逆变化：A hard deadline forces Peter out of paralysis — he must decide NOW, not eventually
- 主角当下目标：Decide whether to act, then execute the sabotage without detection

## 3) 升级危机链（Fichtean 危机递增，至少 3 次）
| 节点 | 危机/冲突 | 代价/风险升级 | 结果/变化 |
|------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| 1 | Peter must pretend normalcy while carrying the secret; Kessler engages him in a warm conversation about the "future they're building" — Peter's resolve wavers | Emotional cost — Peter genuinely likes these people; betraying them feels like betraying the only belonging he's ever had | Peter nearly convinces himself to stay silent; his passivity (core flaw) fights his conscience |
| 2 | The code review: Vasquez requests a technical walkthrough of Peter's nav code before it's locked into the system. Peter must present his work while hiding the bug he hasn't yet inserted | Professional risk — if Vasquez spots anything anomalous in his approach, the window closes | Peter passes the review; but now the code is "clean" — he must insert the bug AFTER the review, in the final commit |
| 3 | The insertion: Peter writes the conditional and commits the code in the narrow window between review and lockdown. 12 seconds of typing that decide humanity's fate | Maximum risk — detection means death; failure means human extinction | The bug is in. Peter cannot take it back. The code is locked. |
| 4 | Launch day: Peter watches the nanomachine enter the black hole, carrying his sabotage 67 million years into the past. He must celebrate with the crew while knowing what he's done | Existential — the act is irreversible; Peter's moral isolation is complete; he has altered the course of deep time and no one knows | The nanomachine is gone. The bug is 67 million years ago. Everything now depends on whether Peter's code executes correctly across eons of autonomous operation. |

## 4) 中段反转（必填）
- 假胜利/假失败：The launch succeeds perfectly. Every technical parameter is nominal. The crew celebrates. Kessler is moved to tears. Peter is praised for his brilliant trajectory work. From the group's perspective, years of planning have culminated in a flawless execution. From Peter's perspective, this is the moment his sabotage became irreversible — not a victory but a point of no return.
- 反转带来的新认知：Peter realizes he can never tell anyone what he's done. Whether the bug works or not, he must live with this alone. If it works, he saved humanity but condemned the dinosaurs (again). If it fails, he tried and humanity is gone. Either way, he can never explain, never be thanked, never be absolved.

## 5) 卷末最低谷（All Is Lost）
- 最低谷事件：The group monitors Earth scans for changes to the Chicxulub crater. Hours pass. Days pass. Nothing changes. The crater is still there. The geological record is unchanged. The mission failed. Kessler is shattered. The Handler demands a systems review. Every component checked out — except the navigation code. All eyes turn to Peter.
- 代价：Peter is now a suspect in a room full of people who have already killed one person (Mike Reeves, Ch4) and who have nothing left to lose
- 主角选择：Peter must survive — deny, deflect, or escape — knowing he is the only person alive who knows why the mission failed

## 6) 卷末大兑现 + 新钩子（Payoff + Next Promise）
- 本卷兑现：
  1. Peter's character arc completes: passive → decisive (the bug insertion is the climax of his transformation)
  2. The 0.01 → -0.01 reversal: the story's signature concept delivered in full — one conditional statement saved humanity
  3. "Nothing changed": the most understated climax possible — reality simply didn't move, and that IS the victory
  4. Every foreshadow pays off: nanomachines (Ch2), accelerator (Ch3), Sun 2.0 (Ch4), Peter's expertise (Ch5), the cover story (Ch8), the star chart (Ch10) — all converge on a 7-millisecond black hole window
- 新钩子：Peter is cornered. The group knows the mission failed. The only unexplained variable is the navigation code. The Professor who treated Peter like a son now looks at him with dawning suspicion. Peter has no escape route, no allies, and no way off the asteroid.
- 章末未闭合问题：Will Peter survive? What will Kessler do when he confirms Peter's betrayal?
