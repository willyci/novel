# 反派设计

## 基本信息
- 名称：Radical Animal Rights Group (leader TBD — possibly the mysterious man in shadows)
- 身份/势力：Underground extremist cell operating from Kuiper Belt
- 出场时机：Their agents appear from Ch1 (mysterious stranger at museum) onward

| 大反派 | Professor Arthur Kessler | Mid-Late (Ch7+) | Execute the time travel plan to erase humanity; designed the cover story | Peter's ultimate adversary and philosophical mirror |

## 核心驱动
- 核心欲望：Undo humanity's destruction of Earth; save animal life (specifically dinosaurs as symbol)
- 核心恐惧：Humanity will continue destroying all life if allowed to exist
- 行动原则：The ends justify the means — willing to erase billions of humans to "save" animals

## 镜像对抗
- 与主角共享欲望/缺陷：Both believe the current world is broken and want to fix it
- 反派道路：They choose extinction (erase humanity); Peter chooses preservation (protect humanity through sabotage)
- 价值观冲突点：Is human existence worth preserving given its destruction of the environment? The antagonists say no; Peter says yes

## 能力与资源
- 实力层级：Highly organized; access to black market networks, false identities, scientific knowledge
- 关键能力/手段：
  - Social engineering (manipulating Steve, hiring Peter under false pretenses)
  - Technical expertise (activating dormant nanomachines, operating particle accelerator)
  - Operational security (DNA trace burning, compartmentalized knowledge)
- 组织/资源支持：Secret lab on Kuiper Belt asteroid; enough funding for black market purchases and hiring specialists

## 规则与代价
- 被限制的规则：Need specific rare tech (Sun 2.0, accelerator, nanomachines) — can't create from scratch
- 代价机制：The heist itself is high-risk; lab explosion (National Energy Lab) caused a casualty (Mike the security guard)
- 反制点（主角可利用）：They MUST hire an outside expert (Peter) for navigation code — creating their vulnerability

## 关键剧情节点
- 第一次正面交锋：Peter realizes the star positions are 67 million years old
- 中期压制点：Peter is trapped — working for them with no easy way out
- 失败/反转节点：The nanomachine is sent through the black hole, but Peter's bug reverses the trajectory
- 终局命运：Plan fails; they realize immediately and begin plotting a new, more sinister scheme

## 复杂度
- 是否可洗白/转化：Philosophically sympathetic (they're not wrong about environmental destruction) but methods are inexcusable
- 是否存在更高层反派：Possible — "even more sinister plan" teased at the end

### The Leader / The Professor — Professor Arthur Kessler
- First appears: Ch7 (as the base's senior scientist); true role as the plan's architect revealed gradually
- Age: ~63 in 2543 (born ~2480 on Mars)

#### Background & Radicalization Arc
**Early career (2500s)**: One of Mars's most respected evolutionary biologists. Full professor at Olympus University by 35. Specialized in the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event — his PhD thesis modeled what would have happened if the Chicxulub asteroid had missed Earth. Beloved by students, cited across disciplines.

**The Earth Expeditions (2510-2522)**: Kessler led five government-funded research expeditions to Earth to document remaining ecosystems before final ecological collapse. He spent a cumulative 5 years on Earth — walking through dying forests, diving dying reefs, cataloging species that would be extinct within a decade. He fell in love with the planet his species had destroyed.

**The personal anchor**: Kessler's grandmother, Miriam, was an Earth-born marine biologist — one of the last generation to grow up with living oceans. She spent her final years on Mars telling young Arthur about the coral reefs, the whale migrations, the bioluminescent tides. She died when he was 12. Her stories shaped his entire worldview. On his third Earth expedition, Kessler found her childhood village — a coastal settlement now three meters underwater. He retrieved a fragment of dead coral from the ruins. He keeps it on his desk. It is his totem.

**The Last Reef (2523)**: On his final expedition, Kessler documented the death of Earth's last living coral reef — the Andaman Fragment, a tiny patch of ocean life that had survived everything else. He watched it bleach and die over three weeks. His field notes from this period are legendary in academic circles: raw, precise, furious. He wrote: *"We came from this ocean. We owe everything to the life that evolved here before us. And we killed it all."*

**The Defunding (2525)**: Upon returning to Mars, Kessler proposed a massive Earth conservation initiative backed by his 12 years of field data. The Mars government reviewed his proposal and concluded that Earth's ecosystems were "beyond recovery." They officially reclassified Earth as a "resource extraction zone" and redirected conservation funding to Mars terraforming projects. Kessler's department was defunded. His colleagues moved to corporate research. His students scattered.

**Academic exile (2525-2535)**: Kessler became increasingly vocal — publishing papers arguing that humanity's treatment of Earth was not merely an environmental failure but a moral catastrophe. He was pushed out of mainstream academia, labeled a "radical ecologist." Lost his tenure. Lost his lab. The university politely suggested early retirement. His wife left him during this period (she wanted stability; he wanted to burn institutions down). He ended up in a rented lab with borrowed equipment and no funding.

**The Intellectual Breakthrough (2535)**: Alone and discredited, Kessler was reviewing his old thesis models — the "asteroid miss" scenarios — when the insight hit him. The Chicxulub impact wasn't just the end of the dinosaurs. It was the *beginning* of humanity. Every extinction, every destroyed ecosystem, every species humanity had wiped out traced back to a single random cosmic event 67 million years ago. A rock hit the planet at the wrong angle. If it had deviated by 0.01 degrees, it would have missed. The dinosaurs would still be evolving. The most magnificent biosphere in the universe would still be alive. And the species that destroyed it would never have existed.

**Building the plan (2536-2543)**: Over 7 years, Kessler assembled the team, identified the required technologies (nanomachines, particle accelerator, Sun 2.0 energy source), and designed the operational plan. He recruited his two most devoted former students. He brought in the Handler (a former student's contact with black market connections) to manage logistics and acquisitions. He designed the cover story himself — the "time-traveling asteroid mining" concept — drawing on his deep knowledge of mining economics and orbital mechanics.

#### The Philosophical Argument (genuinely hard to refute)
Kessler's position is NOT "humanity deserves to die." It is more subtle and more dangerous:
- The humans who would be "erased" never existed in the alternate timeline. You cannot kill someone who was never born. This is prevention, not murder.
- What he IS doing is restoring 67 million years of evolutionary possibility that was stolen by a random cosmic accident.
- Earth's ecosystems — the most complex, beautiful, and irreplaceable structures in the known universe — were destroyed by a species that arose from a cosmic fluke.
- Humanity had its chance. Five hundred years of industrial capability, and it used that capability to destroy every ecosystem, every species, every ocean, every forest that made its own existence possible.
- The plan isn't revenge. It's correction. Putting the universe back on the trajectory it was supposed to follow before a random asteroid knocked it off course.
- He asks: "If you could undo a car accident that killed a family, would you? Even if the accident had also created something? We don't owe our existence to a moral event. We owe it to a catastrophe. I'm simply un-doing the catastrophe."

#### Personality
- Articulate, fatherly, intellectually generous; makes everyone feel heard
- Genuinely brilliant — designed the cover story, the operational plan, and the science
- Warm in a way that is MORE dangerous than coldness — Peter will genuinely like him
- Does not shout, threaten, or posture; speaks with the quiet conviction of someone who has spent decades arriving at a conclusion
- Sees himself as a doctor performing necessary surgery, not a murderer

#### Mirror to Peter
- Both are brilliant men whose expertise was rejected by society
- Peter was replaced by AI; Kessler was replaced by indifference
- Peter's response: withdrawal and passivity (6 months on the couch)
- Kessler's response: radical action ("if the world won't listen, change the world")
- Kessler represents what Peter COULD become if his disillusionment deepened further
- Peter's opposition to Kessler (Vol 3) is a rejection of a possible future self

#### Backstory Reveal Schedule (Vol 2)
- **Ch7 (surface)**: Peter meets Kessler. Notices the coral fragment on his desk, old Earth expedition photos on the wall. Kessler mentions he "used to teach" — an understatement. Peter senses depth but doesn't probe.
- **Ch8 (expertise)**: During the cover story pitch, Kessler's knowledge of deep-time evolution and asteroid dynamics feels too passionate, too deep for a "mining entrepreneur." His academic pedigree bleeds through the business veneer.
- **Ch9 (the personal story)**: Late night after crew dinner. Kessler and Peter are alone. Kessler opens up — the Earth expeditions, the last reef, grandmother Miriam's stories, the coral fragment, the defunding. He tells Peter: "I spent my life watching humanity destroy the most beautiful thing in the universe. This project is my chance to build something instead." (This is true — just not in the way Peter thinks.) This scene cements the emotional bond that Ch10's revelation will destroy.

#### Key quote directions
- On Earth: "I've walked through forests that took sixty million years to evolve and six decades to die. The trees were still standing when I was there. They just weren't alive anymore."
- On the plan: "We're not destroying anything, Peter. We're restoring what was stolen. Every species humanity exterminated — they get another chance. Sixty-seven million years of evolutionary possibility, returned."
- The unanswerable question (for Peter in Ch10): "What has humanity built that's worth sixty-seven million years of life?"

## The Cover Story (First-Level Lie)
The group tells Peter and possibly other crew members a plausible business rationale:
- They've invented time-traveling nanomachine mining: send nanomachines back millions of years to mine asteroids before competitors exist
- Nanomachines spend eons mining, refining, then launch ingots on million-year trajectories toward Earth
- Orbital interceptors in the present catch the ancient shipments = zero-wait-time mining monopoly
- This explains ALL the stolen tech: nanomachines (mining), accelerator + Sun 2.0 (time travel), navigation code (ingot trajectories)
- The cover story breaks when Peter notices star positions are 67M years old (extinction era, not "millions") and the trajectory targets the Chicxulub asteroid path, not interceptor orbits
