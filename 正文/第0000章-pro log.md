Prologue

An unassuming asteroid collides with its celestial neighbor in the silent void of space. Witnessless and nameless, it loses a few fragments upon impact. A small piece breaks off, drifting toward the inner solar system.

Its trajectory carries it through the cosmic expanse — a mote adrift in the vastness of space. Perhaps it will continue its solitary journey for eons, cold and unnoticed. Or perhaps it will be drawn into a planet's gravitational embrace, becoming a fleeting streak of light in an alien sky.

The fragment tumbles onward, indifferent to its fate. In the grand cosmic ballet, it plays but a tiny, forgettable role — one of countless bits of matter scattered across an indifferent universe.

Its course is set. A fraction of a degree in any direction, and its story would be entirely different.

A drama unfolding in the vast indifference of the universe.
