Chapter 1: Echoes of Extinction
Sarah Parker stood motionless before the colossal dinosaur skull, its empty eye sockets seeming to bore into her very soul. The sight transported her back to happier times—her son Ben's wide-eyed wonder, her husband's enthusiastic explanations. Both of them had shared a passion for these prehistoric beasts. Now, that shared joy was nothing more than a bittersweet memory.
Shaking off the nostalgia, Sarah turned to face her class. The field trip to Earth's Natural History Museum was a rare treat for these Martian-born children. Their altered physiologies—multi-faceted eyes and nictitating membranes—were a stark reminder of how far humanity had come. Or perhaps, how far they'd been forced to go.
"And so, young ones," Sarah's voice carried through the cavernous hall, amplified by the students' neural implants, "this is how the dinosaurs of Old Earth met their end. An asteroid, knocked off course from the ancient Kuiper Belt, collided with the planet, resulting in a catastrophic extinction event."
The holographic display flickered to life, showing Earth's violent collision with the massive space rock. The children gasped collectively, their augmented minds struggling to comprehend the scale of such destruction.
"Why did the asteroid fly toward Earth?" piped up a voice from the back.
Sarah smiled, recognizing the insatiable curiosity of Little Timmy, a child gifted with an extra brain lobe for enhanced problem-solving. "Well, Timmy, there are thousands of asteroids in the Kuiper Belt, constantly colliding with each other. This particular event was a one-in-a-million occurrence—just the right place, at just the right time."
Timmy's tendrils waved in thought before he asked, "But Ms. Parker, what if the asteroid had missed Earth?"
The question caught Sarah off guard. Her bioluminescent patterns flickered, betraying her surprise. "Well, I... I'm not entirely certain, Timmy. I've never really considered it. I suppose a lot of things could have changed?"

Before Sarah could elaborate, a deep, resonant voice cut through the air. "If that asteroid had missed, those magnificent beasts would still be roaming our planet. Earth would be a lush, verdant paradise—quite unlike the environmental challenges we face today."
Sarah whirled around, startled by the interruption. A tall man in a rumpled tweed jacket stood nearby, his eyes twinkling with an odd light.
The stranger's words hung in the air as the children exchanged excited glances, their young minds racing with visions of an alternate Earth.
A sudden quietness fell over the kids, broken only by the stranger's dry laugh. "It's just one of a million possibilities!" he added, his voice tinged with an emotion Sarah couldn't quite place.
Shaking off her momentary confusion, Sarah clapped her hands to regain her students' attention. "All right, kids, let's keep moving. We have a lot more to see before we leave!" She began ushering the children towards the next exhibition, her teacher instincts kicking in despite the odd interruption.
As the class moved on, Sarah noticed Timmy lagging behind, his hand outstretched as if trying to catch the holographic asteroid. The sight brought a small smile to her face, reminding her of Ben's endless fascination with space.
"Mia, try to catch up with the class!" Sarah called out to a girl who had paused to examine a nearby display. As she herded the stragglers forward, Sarah didn't realize that she had already forgotten what the stranger looked like.
