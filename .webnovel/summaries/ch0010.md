## Plot Summary

The progress bar opens at 4%. Peter walks away. Sol 58→65 compresses through the routine — Vasquez integration window, galley meals he does not remember, ten-second checks at the Nav Lab — until the bar reads 100%. He waits to Sol 66 0220, dimmed corridor cycle, gray-flour bay door shut, and reads the result line three times. Reference epoch barycentric center: sixty-seven million years. The Cretaceous. He reruns the query twice (frame inverted, naive) — same. The named-event field is populated. He pulls the catch-side-pass-1 trajectory geometry and runs it the other way. In the inverted frame the same vectors describe an asteroid falling on Earth and a body in close station-keeping deflecting it; the integration tolerance Vasquez had called "higher than you'll need" is the Casimir-cradle headroom for CTC injection. The 0.01-degree adjustment is not a launch correction — it is the deflection that makes the asteroid miss Earth, the entire point of his code. The catalogue's named-event field is tagged Chicxulub. There are no orbital interceptors. There never were. Implication lands in two stages: the impactor never falls; humanity never evolves. Sol 67 day cycle: he answers Vasquez's questions in cradle-handshake-timing language, eats stew with Oren, performs a man's words, and at the Nav Lab late afternoon recontextualizes the coral fragment — not Miriam memento but engine, the totem of grief converted into the appropriate measure. He passes Kessler's closed door, warm strip of light beneath, and does not knock. Sol 68 0140: Nav Lab, cache deletion (query history, back-derived trajectory file, lookup logs, runtime artifacts), catch-side-pass-1 left untouched. Screen corner blank. Bunk. Observation port shows the Sol 68 stars exactly where they should be — for now. Peter has the knowledge, the access, and the one skill. He does not have the certainty. Not yet.
