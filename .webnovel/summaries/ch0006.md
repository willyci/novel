## Plot Summary
After 14 days in a cargo shuttle piloted by Diaz, Peter arrives at a hollowed-out asteroid facility in the Kuiper Belt. Samir -- the unnamed interviewer from Ch5 -- greets him as his project supervisor. During a tour, Peter observes an accelerator lab (Dr. Vasquez's domain), a nanomachine programming bay, and a section radiating intense heat and static (all three stolen technologies from Ch2-4). The base has no corporate logos, no external comms except twice-weekly relay, and houses a team of nine. Peter meets Hiro, the Machine Tech. In the Nav Lab, Peter finds military-grade software and a dedicated stellar cartography database, with his workstation pre-configured to his preferences. Opening the project specs, he discovers navigation parameters far beyond any mining application: dozens of stellar reference epochs, extreme precision tolerances, gravitational models for unknown bodies, and autonomous operation windows of extraordinary duration. He recognizes the pattern is wrong but chooses not to ask questions yet.
