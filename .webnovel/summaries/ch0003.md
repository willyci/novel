## Plot Summary
Marcus Webb, the 11-year facility manager of the aging Prometheus Particle Research Facility, sells the Kepler-7 particle accelerator to a man posing as Dr. Freeman, an associate of the reputable Dr. Hadron. Despite Freeman's convincing technical knowledge and legitimate-looking credentials, Marcus notices troubling signs: Freeman refuses the recommended transport company and twice deflects requests for an installation address. Financial pressure from Helios Corp's decommission threats overrides Marcus's instincts. After the sale, Freeman removes his prosthetic disguise, revealing a younger face, and reports to an unknown handler that the accelerator is secured and bound for the outer solar system. Marcus files a regulatory inquiry, but it will take weeks to process -- by then, the accelerator and its ability to generate micro black holes will be long gone.
