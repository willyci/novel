## 剧情摘要

Sol 70 0640: Peter eats paste at the galley, two days past the night his hands had stopped. Sol 68 and 69 have passed without incident; he delivered the catch-side-pass-1 rebalance to Kessler. The screen-corner where the warning flag and progress bar lived is blank; the unwritten conditional has been the shape of a thing in the next room for forty-eight hours and has not gotten smaller. Oren arrives at 0715 with grey coffee and dry humor about Vasquez using "tolerance" twice; Peter privately decides he is going to remember the blue stain on the cup. At 1000 Kessler calls the whole base to the briefing room — coral fragment in hand — and announces D-10: Sol 80, 0413 base time, the Cauchy window opens for seven milliseconds; on Sol 80 they inject. Vasquez intercepts Peter in the corridor and schedules a full nav-stack walkthrough for Sol 75 at 1400. Five days. Late afternoon, Kessler calls Peter to his office and tells him he argued for the unified catch-side spec against the team — that he is glad it was Peter who took it. Peter watches Kessler's hand rest steady near the coral fragment "the way a man's hand lay near a child he was not going to wake," sees a man in his last week of being unopposed, and the conditional is no longer a shape in the next room. Back in the Nav Lab, Peter reads the launch trajectory phase the way Vasquez would and notes — without writing it down, having practice in not writing things down — three places where a small adjustment to the integrator's reference-frame priority would propagate downstream undetected through her review. Dinner with Oren and Diaz; one laugh at Oren's face when dessert is paste with sugar. 2210 close-of-day, blank screen corner, lamp out. The observation port shows the same wedge of stars from Sol 68 advanced two days. He has the knowledge, the access, the one skill, the deadline. He does not have a plan yet. He has five days to write one. He goes to his bunk.
