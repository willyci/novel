## 剧情摘要
Five days of postponement (Sol 45-49) compress before Peter commits on Sol 50 and walks to Kessler's office. Standing, he delivers three rehearsed questions about why the nav code requires stellar drift across millions of years. Kessler — pleased, unhurried — invites him to sit and delivers the project's full cover story: a small Kerr black hole held in a Casimir-stabilized exotic-matter cradle (publicly nicknamed Sun 2.0), opened by Vasquez's cradle parameters and Peter's nav code through a 7ms Cauchy-horizon window into a closed timelike curve, depositing one 0.1g von Neumann nanomachine several million years deep. The seed replicates into a swarm that mines, refines, casts ingots, and launches them on million-year ballistic trajectories caught in the present by distributed orbital interceptors (inner-Kuiper, near-Earth, L4/L5). Zero wait time. The bizarre spec is reframed: launch trajectory phase + interceptor approach geometry. Peter's ribs warm; he does not intercept the warmth. Kessler welcomes him to "the actual project," defers a "third thing," and proposes dinner. Returning past the gray-flour bay, Peter looks at the nano programmer's swarm-in-construction with admiration, not unease. At his workstation he opens a new branch, *catch-side-pass-1*, and refactors cleanly under the new model. The soft yellow warning flag clears; he does not notice it go.
