## 剧情摘要
Eight days compress into integration: catch-side-pass-1 is nearly complete, Vasquez has folded Peter's timing tweak into the cradle, Oren saves him a galley seat, and the soft yellow warning flag is gone from his left display. On Sol 58 Kessler pulls him to dinner with six of nine crew (Oren teases his branch-naming, Vasquez almost-smiles), then walks him to his office and tells him the long answer — Earth six times, his marine-biologist grandmother Miriam (whose village relic the coral fragment is), the Andaman Fragment's death, the 2525 Mars Government reclassification that defunded him, his wife leaving in 2527, the Tharsis basement lab, the 2535 private donor offer, and the mirror line: "I spent my life watching humanity destroy the most beautiful thing in the universe. This project is my chance to build something instead." Peter privately recognizes Kessler as a possible future self, says only "Thank you for telling me," and walks back to the Nav Lab past the closed gray-flour bay door. He runs his routine pre-bed consistency check; the cluster holds. Then on a habit of attention he asks a different question — and finds the reference epochs are not distributed across the cover-story's million-year envelope but clustered tightly around a single point in deep time. A line, not a smear. He pulls down the stellar-cartography comparison tool he hasn't run since Helix, queries the cluster's barycentric center against historical epoch catalogues on his eight-year-old datapad, and a small progress bar opens — quietly, where the warning flag used to live — and begins to load.
